# Requirements Definition  
Discord Community Activity Visualization Platform (Phase 1)

---

## 1. Project Overview

### 1.1 Project Name (Tentative)
Discord Community Activity Visualization Platform

### 1.2 Background and Challenges
The initiator of this project manages a university esports community on Discord with more than 600 members.  
While organizing regular events and maintaining community engagement are essential, key decisions—such as determining the optimal day and time to maximize participation—are currently made based largely on experience and intuition.

In practice, trends that contradict common assumptions (e.g., weekends attract more participants) have been observed.  
However, there is no mechanism to systematically record, verify, or share such observations using quantitative data.

### 1.3 Project Objective
The objective of this project is to collect, store, and visualize user activity data from a Discord server, enabling community management decisions to be made based on data rather than personal experience or intuition.

### 1.4 Positioning for Jedai
This project will be presented at Jedai as a practical case study of building a data platform and visualization pipeline using Databricks for non-business data sources, specifically Discord activity logs.

## 2. As-Is / To-Be

### 2.1 As-Is (Current State)

#### Situation Awareness
- The level of community activity is understood subjectively
- No quantitative records are maintained
- Decisions are often based on impressions such as “it feels less active recently”

#### Hypothesis Formation
- Hypotheses are formed based on past experience or general assumptions  
  - e.g., weekends tend to attract more participants
- There is no way to validate these hypotheses with data

#### Planning and Decision-Making
- Event schedules and content are decided manually
- The rationale behind decisions cannot be explained quantitatively

#### Retrospective
- Success or failure is judged based on impressions
- Learnings are not accumulated as data

---

### 2.2 To-Be (After Phase 1 Completion)

#### Situation Awareness
- Discord activity data is continuously collected and stored
- Activity trends by day of week and time of day can be viewed via dashboards

#### Hypothesis Formation
- Data-driven hypotheses such as “this time slot attracts more participants” can be formed
- Discrepancies between intuition and actual data can be identified

#### Planning and Decision-Making
- Event schedules can be selected based on expected participation
- Decision rationales can be shared with other community organizers

#### Retrospective
- Changes in activity before and after events can be evaluated quantitatively
- Insights can be applied to future planning decisions

## 10. Definition of Community Leader Capabilities

In this project, the role of a *Community Leader* is not treated as a single, monolithic responsibility.  
Instead, it is decomposed into a set of core capabilities commonly observed in job descriptions for community-related roles in game and online service companies (e.g., Community Manager, LiveOps, Player Experience).

These capabilities represent the fundamental functions required to sustainably operate and grow a community.

### 10.1 Community Leader Capability Model

The Community Leader role is decomposed into the following five capabilities:

1. **Observation**  
2. **Analysis & Insight**  
3. **Decision Making**  
4. **Execution & Communication**  
5. **Evaluation & Learning**

This decomposition is designed to be mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive (MECE).

---

### 10.2 Capability Definitions

#### 1. Observation
Continuously monitoring community activity and engagement signals.

Examples:
- Tracking when users are active
- Monitoring participation levels across time periods
- Detecting changes in activity volume

#### 2. Analysis & Insight
Interpreting observed data to identify trends, patterns, and anomalies.

Examples:
- Identifying time slots with higher engagement
- Detecting discrepancies between assumptions and actual behavior
- Formulating data-driven hypotheses

#### 3. Decision Making
Selecting actions and priorities under uncertainty.

Examples:
- Choosing event schedules and formats
- Prioritizing initiatives based on expected impact

#### 4. Execution & Communication
Executing decisions and interacting with the community.

Examples:
- Announcing events
- Communicating with members and moderators
- Coordinating community activities

#### 5. Evaluation & Learning
Assessing outcomes and incorporating learnings into future actions.

Examples:
- Measuring the impact of events
- Conducting retrospectives
- Refining future decisions based on past results

---

### 10.3 Scope of Phase 1 in Relation to Community Leader Capabilities

Phase 1 of this project focuses on systemically supporting a subset of Community Leader capabilities.

Specifically:

- **Observation** is directly supported by automated data collection from the Discord API and continuous storage in Databricks.
- **Analysis & Insight** is partially supported through aggregation and visualization via dashboards.
- **Decision Making**, **Execution & Communication**, and **Evaluation & Learning** remain human-driven in Phase 1, with the system providing informational support only.

This positioning ensures that Phase 1 serves as a foundational layer that augments human decision-making rather than replacing it.
